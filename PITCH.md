# NEMO CrudeFlow: The Pitch

## 🔴 The Problem: The $40,000-per-day Blind Spot

Maritime logistics for crude oil is currently facing a "perfect storm" of volatility. For a typical oil refinery, the logistics of crude intake is a high-stakes balancing act plagued by two massive issues:

### 1. The Demurrage Drain
When a tanker sits idle outside a port because of a scheduling mismatch or congestion, it incurs **Demurrage**. For large vessels (VLCCs), these penalties can range from **$20,000 to $40,000 per day**. For an enterprise refinery, this represents a multi-million dollar annual leak that is often treated as an "unavoidable cost of doing business."
*   **Source**: [Hydrocarbon Engineering: Optimizing Refinery Logistics](https://www.hydrocarbonengineering.com/)

### 2. Geopolitical Rerouting & Supply Volatility
India imports over **85% of its crude oil requirements**. Recent disruptions in the Red Sea have forced vessels to divert around the Cape of Good Hope, adding **14 to 20 days** to transit times and increasing freight costs by **35% to 60%**.
*   **Source**: [S&P Global: Maritime Disruptions & Energy Security](https://www.spglobal.com/)

## 🔵 The Solution: NEMO CrudeFlow

**Neural Engine for Maritime Operations (NEMO)** is not just a tracking tool; it is a **Decision Intelligence Platform**. It bridges the gap between terminal operations and maritime logistics.
